Knowledge & Grace Program

Saturday, 31st May 2025 | Government Girls College, Dukpa, Gwagwalada, FCT, Nigeria

On Saturday, 31st May 2025, Faith Sisters Global Mission held her first inaugural outreach for the year the Education Outreach: Knowledge & Grace Program at Government Girls College, Dukpa, Gwagwalada, FCT, Nigeria.

This transformative outreach empowered young female students to grow academically, emotionally, and spiritually. Through sessions on technology, innovation, and faith, the girls were reminded that true success requires both knowledge and grace. A central teaching on “Increasing in Knowledge and Grace” emphasized identity in Christ, personal growth, and walking boldly in purpose, even amid life's challenges.

A special highlight of the outreach was the presentation of the Faith Sisters Scholarship Award to fourteen (14) exceptional students — a carefully selected mix of high-achievers, deserving girls, and rising talents from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. The award honored more than academic success; it celebrated resilience, leadership potential, and a deep desire for personal growth. It stood as a beacon of hope, affirming that with diligence, grace, and faith, any girl can rise above limitations and achieve greatness. The entire school community expressed heartfelt appreciation, finding renewed encouragement and inspiration in the message and impact of the program. Faith Sisters Global is thankful to our phenomenal members, committed sponsors and partners whose generosity and support continue to make outreaches like this possible.

As always, Faith Sisters Global Mission remains devoted to spreading the gospel and empowering lives from schools to communities raising young women who shine with knowledge, grace, and purpose.

Story Highlights

  • The outreach equipped students through teaching on technology, innovation, faith, identity in Christ, and personal growth.
  • Fourteen exceptional students received the Faith Sisters Scholarship Award as a celebration of resilience, promise, and leadership potential.
  • The program renewed hope across the school community and reflected the mission to raise young women who shine with knowledge, grace, and purpose.
